5-Minute Presentation Guideline

The Pioneers & Future of ICTs

A strategic roadmap for bridging history with future innovation. Follow these 6 steps to deliver an impactful, concise presentation.

The Personal Spark

1 The "Why"

Briefly introduce yourself and share why you chose this specific woman or project. What was the personal spark or "Aha!" moment that drew you to her story?

Setting the Stage

2 The Background

Who is she? Provide a "nutshell" biography that sets the stage for her journey and the specific historical era in which she worked.

Overcoming Barriers

3 Struggle & Resilience

What were the biggest challenges or barriers she faced—whether social, technical, or personal? How did she overcome them to keep moving forward?

The Breakthrough

4 Major Accomplishments

What was her breakthrough in Science, Technology, or ICTs? How does her work help our society today? (Example: How Gladys West’s work paved the way for the GPS we use every day).

Stepping Stones

5 Legacy & The Next Gen

Even if the technology is no longer in active use, how did it "set the ground" or act as a stepping stone for the next generation of inventors and current digital applications?

Looking Forward

6 Your Personal Vision

What is the one core lesson from her life or this project that you will carry into your own future in Science and Technology?

"If I have seen further, it is by standing on the shoulders of giants."
